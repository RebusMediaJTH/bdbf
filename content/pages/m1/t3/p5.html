3 simple rules when using social media

We have three very simple rules for our people when using social media:

  • Rule #1 — Always show respect
  • Rule #2 — Be open
  • Rule #3 — Take personal responsibility

Rule #1
Always show respect

  • Respect others and don't post anything that could be deemed obscene, abusive, illegal, offensive or discriminatory.
  • Whether or not you intended to offend or insult someone is irrelevant.
  • Don't disclose personal or confidential information or do anything that could potentially harm APG's interests.

Rule #2
Be open

  • Identify yourself and your relationship to APG.
  • Declare to others that you are not authorised to speak on behalf of APG.

Rule #3
Take personal responsibility

  • Be mindful that you have legal obligations under copyright, privacy, defamation, competition and consumer, workplace relations, workplace health and safety and other applicable laws and act appropriately.
  • You also have a responsibility when expressing your opinion to ensure statements made on social media forums are true and correct and not misleading.
  • Information, comments and conversations, photos and videos shared with your networks and friends may appear to be private, but in fact can easily become public information. So be mindful of what you post and consider the impact it may have on our business or those you work with.
  • A good reminder is to refrain from posting anything that you would not be willing to post on an APG noticeboard or say to your manager, supervisor or colleague.

Our Social Media policy and guidelines apply to social media contributions that relate to Australia Post Group, our products and services, or our people, partners, customers and competitors.

If we don't think you're showing respect, being open, or taking responsibility, we'll need you to remove your comment and you may be subject to disciplinary action including dismissal.

Spot the difference

You're right, if you said the following...

Jill's at home using social media. That means she can use it as much as she wants, be on it as long as she wants, and she can be unproductive.

Gina is working - her aim is to be productive and only use it very briefly. (Not using it at all is fine too!) She's also being monitored by APG.
