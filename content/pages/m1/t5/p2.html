What is harassment?

You’re more than likely harassing someone if:

  • you make fun of their disability, sex, race, colour, religion or any other traits
  • the other person doesn’t like it and doesn’t return it
  • it offends, insults, humiliates, intimidates or embarrasses, and
  • it’s obvious to most people that what you said or did would be offensive, humiliating or intimidating.

What is sexual harassment?

Sexual Harassment can be something that is said, done, sent, displayed or written that is of a sexual nature and is:

  • an unwelcome sexual advance
  • an unwelcome request for sexual favours
  • other unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature.

Behaviour constitutes sexual harassment if most people would anticipate the possibility that the person being harassed would find it offensive, humiliating or intimidating.
