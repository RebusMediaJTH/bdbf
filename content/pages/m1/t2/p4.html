Tracey's Story

Just after the Christmas and New Year's break I suffered a mental health episode.

To help me get back to work sooner, my manager and I discussed the tasks I normally did and we worked with some of my team members to redesign my role temporarily. We made it so my interactions with customers were minimised until I felt better able to deal with the range of emotions that type of work can involve.

The feeling of being supported and getting back to work was amazing and really helped my recovery.

We all work hard at work so I was a little concerned that some of my team members might not understand the reasons behind the changes to my role. I didn't want a misunderstanding to make me a target for comments and rumours.

I brought this up with my manager and she suggested we arrange a team meeting to ensure everyone understood my circumstances. I was OK with that so long as we didn't go into the details of my condition.

We had the meeting on my first day back and I was blown away by the support and understanding from my team. It was a huge relief.

When I was at my lowest point (and my pride was preventing me from seeking help) it was my co-workers that intervened and helped me get help.

Later that year, the relationship I had been in had become quite stressful. It wasn't until someone at work noticed something was up and asked me if I was OK that I just broke down and opened up about what was going on.

I was in denial, I didn't want to face it and I was so embarrassed by it all. So my co-worker spoke with my manager confidentially and came back and told me that I should discuss it with someone who may be able to help.

I was able to access leave which gave me the time to move out and find a new place. I honestly don't know how I would have done it without the support I got at work.

Tracey was able to access support when she needed it and this reduced the amount of harm she suffered.

Australia Post Group staff

At APG, we provide support for victims of domestic violence or those who need to care for a family member in that situation through:

  • Domestic or family violence leave
  • Flexible working arrangements
  • Support services

We even provide support services for workers who recognise their behaviour at home is a problem.

You can talk to:

  • a co-worker or a People Leader you trust,
  • your Human Resources Business Partner, or
  • The Employee Assistance Program on 1300 360 364.

Everyone else

You can talk to:

  • a co-worker or a People Leader you trust, or
  • your GP Doctor for both physical and mental health matters.
