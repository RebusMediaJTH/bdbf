It's the same for our people at work

The strength of our safety culture rests on our ability to communicate about safety freely and without fear.

Potential causes of harm that we keep a look out for include:

  • Workplace hazards (including Dangerous Goods)
  • Alcohol and drug abuse
  • Harassment, discrimination and bullying
  • Violence and other types of assault at work and at home
  • Risks to physical security
Safety's an ongoing conversation here. We're all involved and we're always improvinG... Everyone, Everywhere, Everyday.

...But it's not just about manual handling and Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) and safe work procedures...

Sometimes the hazards at work are difficult to see.

We need to be able to identify all hazards, assess their risks, and either eliminate them or reduce exposure to them.

The cause of Carl's behaviour isn't obvious; what could it be?

The following are sources of risk because they can cause people to become distracted from their tasks:

  • Harassment
  • Discrimination
  • Bullying
  • Family and domestic violence
  • Use of alcohol and other drugs

It's important that we take the time to look below the surface and think about what might be causing hazardous behaviour at work.

These issues are often sensitive so it's important that we find the right way to communicate about them.

We need to respond promptly to all hazards ...

... the obvious and the not so obvious - as well as any incidents.

This means:

  • Making the right decision on how to manage or report a hazard or incident.
  • Making sure that any injury or illness is given immediate medical attention.
We also help anybody who's returning to work from injury or illness back to a sense of 'normal' work-life as quickly as possible.

Who can you speak to about hazards at work?

If you see a hazard and you are unable to make it safe, contact:

  • Your People Leader

If someone's behaviour at work is creating a hazard or you're a bit concerned about them, you can speak to your:

  • People Leader
  • Health and Safety Representative
  • Human Resources Business Partner

We have many policies to help you make better decisions at work. Should you want to know more about how we can better identify, communicate and consult on risks at work take a look at the core policy overviews at the end of this module.
