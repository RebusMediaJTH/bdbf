What is Personal Information?

Personal Information can be anything such as a name, address, signature or photo that identifies or could identify someone.

Even if someone's identity isn't obvious from a single piece of information - such as an address - it's still considered Personal Information because combining it with other pieces of information could identify someone.

Collecting Personal Information

When collecting Personal Information:

  • Explain why it's being collected and how it may be used
  • Be clear that you're representing Australia Post Group
  • Only ask for information that's necessary for our business or to complete your task
  • Always make sure it's accurate, up-to-date, and complete
  • Only get the information from the customer themselves

Of course, we do all this fairly and within the law.

Using Personal Information

After collecting the Personal Information:

  • Be careful how you use it
  • Make sure that you have the person's consent
ONLY USE Personal Information for the purpose it was collected.

If you need that same Personal Information for another reason, you need to explain this to the customer and ask for their permission to use it.

Using Personal Information

Make sure that you protect Personal Information. Don't leave it:

  • Lying around at work where anyone can pick it up
  • Visible on a screen

Be careful who you tell

  • If somebody asks for Personal Information, always make sure that you get the right approvals.
  • The general rule is, we don't tell or give anyone Personal Information and only use it for the purpose it was collected.
When customer information is no longer needed, dispose of it securely (not in the bin where anyone can pick it up).
Always stop and think when handling Personal Information.

Tony works as a postie. While delivering mail to a block of flats a man approaches him and says:

"Hi. I'm here visiting my friend. I know she lives in this building because she told me the address, but I've forgotten which apartment number is hers. Can you help me figure it out? Her name's Jane Smith."

How should Tony respond?

Select the best answer and then select Submit.

  • Tony should help the person because it's what we do. He can check the mail he's delivering and see if there's anything for Jane Smith then pass on the apartment number.
  • Tony should politely inform the man that he is unable to help him because disclosing the Personal Information of one of our customers without their consent is against the law.

That's not right.

Tony should stop and think: "What if this person is lying and Jane Smith does not wish for him to know where she lives?"

By disclosing Jane Smith's Personal Information to another person Tony would be breaching our Privacy Policy and breaking the law, which could have serious consequences for both Tony and the Australia Post Group.

That's correct.

We never disclose our customers' Personal Information to a third party without their consent.
